National Symbols of India

    

    

    
    

        
National Flag - Tiranga

        
        
The national flag of India, known as the Tiranga, consists of three horizontal stripes of saffron, white, and green. The Ashoka Chakra, a 24-spoke wheel, is in the center. It was adopted on July 22, 1947. The flag symbolizes courage (saffron), peace (white), and prosperity (green), with the Ashoka Chakra representing righteousness and progress.

    

    

    
    

        
National Emblem - Lion Capital of Ashoka

        
        
The Lion Capital of Ashoka, adopted as the national emblem on January 26, 1950, is a representation of four lions standing back to back. It symbolizes strength, courage, and confidence, reflecting India's rich cultural heritage and its democratic values.

    

    

    
    

        
National Animal - Bengal Tiger

        
        
The Bengal Tiger, declared the national animal of India in 1973, represents strength, grace, and power. It plays a crucial role in India's biodiversity. Conservation efforts under Project Tiger have been implemented to protect this majestic animal.

    

    

    
    

        
National Bird - Indian Peafowl

        
        
The Indian Peafowl, or Peacock, was declared the national bird in 1963. Known for its colorful plumage and magnificent display, it symbolizes beauty, grace, and pride. The peacock is deeply embedded in Indian culture, myths, and traditions.

    

    

    
    

        
National Flower - Lotus

        
        
The lotus, India's national flower, signifies purity and beauty. It holds a sacred place in Indian mythology and is often associated with divine figures. The lotus grows in muddy water but blooms clean and fragrant, symbolizing purity of heart and mind.
